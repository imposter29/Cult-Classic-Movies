TOP 5 CULT CLASSICS

▼

1. Fight Club

Directed by David Fincher

Unhappy with his capitalistic lifestyle, a white-collared insomniac forms an underground fight club with Tyler, a careless soap salesman. Soon, their venture spirals down into something sinister.

2. THE ROCKY HORROR PICTURE SHOW

Directed by Jim Sharman

A newly-engaged couple have a breakdown in an isolated area and must seek shelter at the bizarre residence of Dr. Frank-n-Furter.

3.
Godfather

Directed by

Francis Ford Coppola

The Godfather is an epic tale of power, loyalty, and betrayal, following the Corleone crime family as they navigate the violent and complex world of organized crime. From Vito Corleone's leadership to Michael's reluctant yet ruthless rise, the film explores the bonds of family and the high cost of ambition.

4. American Psycho

Directed by
Mary H Harron

A wealthy New York City investment banking executive, Patrick Bateman, hides his alternate psychopathic ego from his co-workers and friends as he delves deeper into his violent, hedonistic fantasies.

5. Pulp Fiction

Directed by Quentin Tarantino



The lives of two mob hitmen, a boxer, a gangster and his wife, and a pair of diner bandits intertwine in four tales of violence and redemption.
