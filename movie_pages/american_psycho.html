"American Psycho," directed by Mary Harron and based on Bret Easton Ellis's novel, follows Patrick Bateman (Christian Bale), a wealthy New York City investment banker.

Bateman lives a double life, hiding his true nature as a psychopathic killer beneath the surface of a superficial and consumer-driven society.

The film explores themes of vanity, materialism, and the disconnection from humanity, with Bateman's violent tendencies serving as a satirical commentary on the era's greed.

As Bateman's insanity grows, he descends further into chaos, blurring the line between reality and his delusions.

Regarded as a cult classic, "American Psycho" is both a chilling and darkly humorous examination of identity, excess, and societal values in the 1980s.
